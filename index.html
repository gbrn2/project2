Classification of PET and HDPE Bottles for a Greener Planet

LEARN THE FACT

Recycling PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) and HDPE (High-Density Polyethene) plastic bottles is a key solution to reducing global plastic pollution that adversely affects the environment, wildlife, climate, human health, and social justice. Additionally, the recycling process for HDPE and PET has a lower energy consumption than many other forms of plastic recycling or virgin plastic production. According to the EPA, recycling one pound of PET bottles (that’s about 10 two-liter soda bottles) saves approximately 26,000 BTUs of energy. Plastic recycling also reduces production costs. In HDPE, the price of raw materials is $8.50 per kg, but if recycled, it becomes $2.50. In PET, the raw material price ranges from $2.00–$3.00 per kg to $0.80–$1.20 per kg if recycled. PET and HDPE plastic recycling can reduce plastic pellet production costs for packaging beverage companies. Plastic is made from petroleum raw materials, which are non-renewable raw materials and difficult to obtain. Recycling plastic can replace production from non-renewable raw materials.

91% of the world's plastic bottles are not recycled
